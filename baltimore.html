Baltimore

Founded in 1729, is the largest city in the U.S state of Maryland, with population of 622,104 in 2013. It is also the second largest seaport in the Mid-Atlantic. The Inner Harbor was also once the largest leading port of entry for immigrants to the United States. It was also a major manufacturing, industrialization and rail traansportation. Baltimore later became a service oriented city by opening John Hopkins Hospital in 1889 and the John Hopkins University in 1889.

The Baltimore city is named after Cecil Calvert, who was the second Lord Baltimore (1605-1675)

Baltimore played a vital role that lead to the events of the American Revolution. After city leaders joined the reistance to British taxes, and merchants signed agreeements not to trade with Britain anymore. This city was the site of the Battle of Baltimore during the War of 1812. That was when Francis Scott Key witnessed the American Flag on the morning of September 4, 1814 and wrote "The Star Spangled Banner".

Today Baltimore is home to many of Maryland's most popular attractions such as the Orioles baseball stadium, the Ravens football stadium, Walters Art Museaum, The Horseshoe Casino, The Baltimore Zoo, The Baltimore Aquarium, the Inner Harbor, The Ripleys Believe it or Not exhibit and many more. It is also home to one of the most well known and exceptionally high end hospitals in the nation such as John Hopkins Hospital, Mercy Medical Center and the University of Maryland Medical Center. With Hopkins ranked number one in the state.
